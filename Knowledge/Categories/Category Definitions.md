# Category Definitions

## Purpose

This file defines the canonical scrap categories used inside Scrap Helper. Yard-specific wording belongs in `Yard Category Mapping.md`.

## Core Rules

- Use one canonical category per material type.
- Preserve the scrapyard's own label separately.
- Do not assume two similar yard labels pay the same rate.
- Do not merge grades unless the yard confirms they are accepted together.
- A category may remain inactive until confirmed locally.

## Initial Canonical Categories

### Shred Steel

Mixed light ferrous material generally accepted as shred, tin, light iron, or similar low-grade ferrous scrap.

Typical sources:

- appliance shells;
- filing cabinets;
- steel grill bodies;
- light steel housings;
- mixed steel frames.

### Prepared Steel

Ferrous material cut or prepared to yard size and contamination requirements.

Preparation requirements vary by yard.

### Cast Iron

Heavy cast ferrous material such as grates, machine castings, and some appliance components.

### Stainless Steel

Stainless material accepted separately from ordinary steel.

The app must not infer stainless grade from appearance alone.

### Clean Aluminum Wheel

Aluminum vehicle wheel accepted without tire, valve stem, wheel weights, or excessive contamination when required by the yard.

### Cast Aluminum

Cast aluminum components with minimal contamination.

### Sheet Aluminum

Thin formed or sheet aluminum, subject to yard cleanliness rules.

### Electric Motors

Whole electric motors accepted under the yard's motor category.

### Transformers

Whole transformers or transformer-heavy assemblies accepted under the yard's transformer category.

### Insulated Copper Wire

Copper-bearing insulated wire not classified as bare copper.

### Power Cords

Insulated appliance and electronics cords, when the yard separates these from general insulated wire.

### Low Grade Circuit Boards

Low-grade printed circuit boards and controls, subject to local electronic-scrap acceptance.

### Brass

Clean or mixed brass accepted under the yard's brass category.

## Future Categories

Add categories only after one of the following:

- yard confirmation;
- receipt confirmation;
- direct need from a real object template;
- reliable research showing the distinction matters.

Do not add speculative categories merely to make the list look comprehensive.
