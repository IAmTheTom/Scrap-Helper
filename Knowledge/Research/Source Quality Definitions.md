# Source Quality Definitions

## High Quality

Examples:

- manufacturer net-weight specification;
- official service manual;
- government safety guidance;
- actual scrapyard receipt;
- direct controlled measurement with documented method;
- published scrapyard preparation policy.

Suitable for high-confidence claims when the source directly supports the statement.

## Medium Quality

Examples:

- recognized recycler guidance;
- repair documentation;
- several consistent model-specific listings;
- controlled teardown video with clear measurements;
- multiple consistent field reports.

Useful when primary evidence is unavailable, but limitations must be recorded.

## Low Quality

Examples:

- one forum comment;
- unattributed chart;
- generic blog;
- search-result summary without source inspection;
- AI-generated estimate;
- reseller marketing claim;
- anecdotal value estimate.

May guide further research but should not become trusted seed data by itself.

## Unacceptable

Examples:

- unsafe destructive instructions;
- clearly fabricated citations;
- claims contradicted by authoritative sources;
- unlabeled guesses presented as facts;
- copied proprietary content without permission;
- weights that confuse shipping and product weight without disclosure.
