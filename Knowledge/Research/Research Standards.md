# Research Standards

## Purpose

These rules govern how information enters the Scrap Helper knowledge base.

## Source Priority

Prefer sources in this order:

1. Manufacturer specifications and service documentation
2. Government and recognized occupational-safety authorities
3. Scrapyard price sheets, acceptance rules, and receipts
4. Direct measurements and controlled teardowns
5. Industry associations and established recyclers
6. Repair documentation
7. Multiple consistent community reports
8. Single community claims
9. Unsourced estimates

## Numeric Claims

Every numeric claim should identify:

- what was measured;
- the exact subtype or model when known;
- net weight versus shipping weight;
- unit;
- source;
- date;
- confidence.

Do not mix shipping weight, installed weight, component weight, and clean recovered scrap weight.

## Safety Claims

Safety information must use technically accurate language.

Do not:

- repeat destructive scrapping folklore as guidance;
- recommend crushing, smashing, grinding, or drilling hazardous components;
- recommend cutting sealed refrigerant systems;
- imply unplugged equipment produces active microwave radiation;
- assume stored-energy components are discharged.

## Material Yields

Do not invent generic percentages for broad categories such as electronics.

Prefer:

- measured component weights;
- measured same-item batches;
- conservative ranges;
- presence classifications when weight evidence is unavailable.

## Resale Claims

Use model-based screening criteria rather than extremely broad resale-dollar ranges.

## Conflicting Evidence

When sources conflict:

1. Preserve both claims.
2. Record why they may differ.
3. Lower confidence.
4. Move unresolved claims to `Unresolved Claims.md`.
5. Do not average incompatible values without justification.

## Citation Format

Assign each source a Source ID in `Source Register.md`.

Example: `SRC-0001`

Knowledge pages should reference the Source ID beside the supported claim.

## App Seed Eligibility

A claim may enter default app seed data only when:

- the source is recorded;
- confidence is assigned;
- the subtype is clear;
- safety wording has been reviewed;
- contradictory evidence is resolved or represented as a range.
