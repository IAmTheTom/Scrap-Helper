# Propane and Fuel Containers

- **Hazard category:** Pressure, flammable vapor, and residue
- **Severity:** Critical
- **Applies to:** BBQ grills, heaters, torches, fuel equipment, and storage cylinders
- **Status:** Supported

## Summary

Propane cylinders and fuel containers may remain pressurized or contain flammable residue even when they appear empty.

## Required Precautions

- Do not collect or process attached cylinders as ordinary scrap.
- Have the owner remove the cylinder when possible.
- Confirm yard policy before transporting any cylinder.
- Keep cylinders upright and secured.
- Keep them away from ignition sources and enclosed hot spaces.
- Treat fuel tanks and lines as containing residue.

## Prohibited Actions

- Do not cut, drill, crush, puncture, or grind a cylinder.
- Do not remove valves unless qualified and permitted.
- Do not test for fuel with flame.
- Do not place unknown cylinders in mixed metal loads.
- Do not transport leaking cylinders.

## Grill Collection Rule

A grill may be collected only after checking for:

- propane cylinder;
- regulator and hose;
- grease buildup;
- sharp corroded panels;
- unstable side shelves or wheels.

The cylinder must be handled separately under local rules.
