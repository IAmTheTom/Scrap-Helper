# Microwave Safety

- **Hazard category:** Stored electrical energy and specialized components
- **Severity:** Critical
- **Applies to:** Microwave ovens
- **Status:** Supported

## Summary

An unplugged microwave does not actively produce microwave radiation, but internal components may retain dangerous electrical energy. The high-voltage capacitor, transformer, wiring, and sharp sheet-metal enclosure require careful handling.

## Critical Hazards

- High-voltage capacitor may retain charge after unplugging.
- Internal high-voltage wiring may be difficult to identify.
- Sheet-metal edges are often sharp.
- The transformer is heavy and may shift suddenly.
- Ceramic components inside some magnetrons may contain hazardous material if broken.

## Required Precautions

- Unplug the microwave before any work.
- Treat the capacitor as charged unless safely verified otherwise.
- Avoid touching capacitor terminals or exposed high-voltage connections.
- Use insulated tools appropriate to electrical work.
- Wear eye protection and cut-resistant gloves.
- Support the transformer before removing its fasteners.
- Keep the magnetron intact.
- Remove parts with ordinary hand tools only.

## Prohibited Actions

- Do not smash, drill, grind, cut, or crush the magnetron.
- Do not short the capacitor with an improvised metal tool.
- Do not work on a wet microwave.
- Do not power the microwave while opened.
- Do not handle damaged ceramic material.
- Do not continue if the internal electrical layout is unfamiliar or inaccessible.

## Safe Stop Conditions

Stop and seek qualified guidance when:

- the capacitor is damaged;
- terminals are exposed and cannot be safely isolated;
- the magnetron is broken;
- there is fire damage, chemical residue, or severe corrosion;
- safe discharge and verification procedures are not understood.

## Scrap-Relevant Parts

Potentially recoverable parts include:

- power cord;
- transformer;
- fan motor;
- control board;
- steel shell;
- internal wire.

Recovery value varies by yard grade and should not override the safety rules above.
