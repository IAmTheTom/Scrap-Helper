# Heavy Item Handling

- **Hazard category:** Lifting, crushing, and transport
- **Severity:** Significant
- **Applies to:** Appliances, cabinets, grills, ranges, motors, transformers, and dense loads
- **Status:** Supported

## Main Risks

- back and shoulder injury;
- crushed hands or feet;
- tipping loads;
- uncontrolled sliding;
- overloaded truck or trailer;
- unstable storage.

## Required Precautions

- Estimate weight before attempting a lift.
- Use a hand truck, appliance dolly, cart, ramp, or second person when needed.
- Clear the path before moving the item.
- Keep hands away from pinch points.
- Lift from stable structural areas, not doors or trim.
- Remove loose shelves, drawers, and panels before moving when practical.
- Use wheel chocks or blocks on ramps.
- Store tall items against a stable surface.

## One-Person Handling Rule

One-person handling is appropriate only when the item can be controlled through the entire movement, including:

- lifting;
- turning;
- ramp movement;
- loading;
- unloading;
- storage placement.

If control is uncertain, use assistance or equipment.

## Vehicle and Trailer Loading

- Verify payload capacity.
- Keep the heaviest items centered and low.
- Maintain appropriate tongue weight on trailers.
- Strap tall or rolling items independently.
- Do not rely on tailgates, cabinet drawers, or appliance doors as tie-down points.
