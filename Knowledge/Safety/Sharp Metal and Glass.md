# Sharp Metal and Glass

- **Hazard category:** Cuts, punctures, and breakage
- **Severity:** Significant
- **Applies to:** Appliances, cabinets, grills, electronics, doors, shelves, and broken items
- **Status:** Supported

## Recognition

Common hazards include:

- unfinished sheet-metal edges;
- broken glass doors or shelves;
- exposed screws;
- snapped plastic with sharp edges;
- rusted panels;
- spring-loaded clips;
- wire ends.

## Required Precautions

- Wear cut-resistant gloves.
- Wear eye protection.
- Remove loose glass before transport when safe.
- Tape or cover sharp exposed edges.
- Store broken glass in a rigid labeled container.
- Keep hands out of unseen cavities.
- Use tools rather than fingers to move sharp fragments.

## Prohibited Actions

- Do not throw glass into loose mixed scrap.
- Do not leave sheet metal standing unsecured.
- Do not carry sharp panels against the body.
- Do not transport loose broken glass in bags.

## Stop Conditions

Stop when:

- tempered glass is cracked and under tension;
- a glass panel cannot be removed or secured safely;
- hidden sharp edges prevent controlled access;
- the item is contaminated with bodily fluids, chemicals, or unknown residue.
