# General Scrap Safety

- **Hazard category:** General handling
- **Severity:** Significant
- **Applies to:** All collection, loading, dismantling, sorting, and transport
- **Status:** Supported

## Summary

Scrap handling combines sharp metal, unstable loads, electrical parts, contaminated surfaces, heavy objects, and uncertain material composition. The value of an item never justifies unsafe handling or processing.

## Required Precautions

- Wear work gloves appropriate to sharp metal.
- Wear eye protection during dismantling.
- Use closed-toe footwear with durable soles.
- Keep a first-aid kit available.
- Use proper lifting technique and mechanical help when needed.
- Stabilize items before removing fasteners or heavy components.
- Keep children and pets away from the work area.
- Keep work areas dry and adequately lit.
- Wash hands after handling scrap and before eating.
- Keep sorted materials in stable containers.
- Secure every load before transport.

## Prohibited Actions

- Do not burn insulation, coatings, plastics, or wire.
- Do not grind or crush unknown materials.
- Do not cut sealed refrigerant systems.
- Do not process propane cylinders or other pressurized vessels.
- Do not bypass yard rules.
- Do not assume electrical equipment is safe because it is unplugged.
- Do not dismantle unstable stacked items.
- Do not leave sharp edges exposed in walkways or transport areas.

## Stop Conditions

Stop processing when:

- the item contains unknown chemicals;
- a sealed pressure vessel is present;
- refrigerant lines are present and intact;
- insulation or dust appears hazardous;
- stored electrical energy cannot be safely verified;
- the item is too heavy or unstable to control;
- the work requires destructive methods outside normal hand-tool disassembly.

## Transport

- Keep dense material low in the bed or trailer.
- Balance loads left-to-right.
- Do not exceed vehicle, tire, hitch, or trailer ratings.
- Strap appliances and tall cabinets independently.
- Prevent loose wire, sheet metal, and panels from leaving the load.
- Recheck straps after a short distance.
